# AIFS ENS (Artificial Intelligence Forecasting System – Ensemble)

## What this model is
AIFS ENS is ECMWF's operational machine-learning-based global ensemble weather forecast system.

Like the deterministic AIFS Single, AIFS ENS is a trained neural network rather than a physics-based solver. However, AIFS ENS is specifically trained to produce probabilistic forecasts using a Continuous Ranked Probability Score (CRPS) loss function, and generates ensemble members by injecting random noise into the transformer processor during inference.

AIFS ENS runs operationally alongside the physics-based IFS ENS and is designed to complement rather than replace it.

---

## Who runs it
- **Organization:** European Centre for Medium-Range Weather Forecasts
- **Country / region:** International (European consortium)

---

## What area it covers
- **Coverage:** Global

---

## Basic details
- **Model type:** Global ensemble (AI-based)
- **Architecture:** Encoder–processor–decoder with attention-based graph neural networks (encoder/decoder) and a sliding-window transformer (processor)
- **Training approach:** CRPS-based probabilistic training
- **Training data:** 38 years of ERA5 reanalysis (1979–2017) plus 8 years of ECMWF operational analyses (2016–2023)
- **Training framework:** Anemoi
- **Model parameters:** ~229 million
- **Spatial resolution:** ~0.25° (Open Data); native resolution approximately 30–31 km
- **Forecast timestep:** 6 hours
- **Number of members:** 51 (1 control + 50 perturbed members)
- **Forecast length:** Up to 15 days
- **Update frequency:** 2× daily (00, 12 UTC); additional cycles at 06 and 18 UTC for some products
- **Initialization:** ECMWF operational analyses (shared with AIFS Single and IFS)

---

## Ensemble methodology
Ensemble members are generated by injecting random Gaussian noise into the transformer processor during inference. Each member uses a different random seed, and members are computed independently — the ensemble is therefore fully parallelizable across GPUs.

Unlike physics-based ensembles (which require separately perturbed analyses and stochastic physics schemes run over full atmospheric model integrations), AIFS ENS produces each ensemble member from a single network evaluation per timestep, making the system dramatically cheaper to run.

The CRPS training approach is designed to produce ensembles with realistic spread and atmospheric variability, avoiding the over-smoothing problem common in deterministically-trained (MSE) AI forecast models.

---

## What it provides
Probabilistic global forecasts of:
- Temperature (surface and upper-air pressure levels)
- Wind (10 m and upper-air)
- Geopotential height
- Specific humidity
- Mean sea level pressure
- Total precipitation
- Additional surface variables
- Ensemble mean, spread, and percentile-based products

AIFS ENS has shown skill improvements of up to 25% over IFS ENS for upper-air variables, with positive impacts on surface variables including 2 m temperature and total precipitation.

---

## Relationship to other models
AIFS ENS is the **ensemble AI companion** to ECMWF's physics-based IFS ENS. It runs operationally alongside IFS ENS and is considered complementary rather than a replacement.

It is the probabilistically-trained counterpart to **AIFS Single** (the deterministic AI model). Both share the same underlying encoder-processor-decoder architecture but differ in training methodology.

AIFS ENS shares **initial conditions with IFS** — it relies on ECMWF's physics-based data assimilation system to generate the starting analysis, then propagates forecasts forward using the neural network.

## Version history

### AIFS ENS v1 — operational July 1, 2025
First operational version of the AIFS ensemble. Replaced earlier research configurations including AIFS–Diffusion (12-hour timestep) and early AIFS–CRPS (6-hour timestep).

### AIFS ENS v2 — scheduled May 12, 2026
Adds a new **10 hPa pressure level** to the stratospheric component and introduces a new **wave stream** (the first coupled AIFS ensemble wave component). Deploys on the same day as IFS Cycle 50r1 and AIFS Single v2.

## Notes
- AIFS ENS generates forecasts more than 10× faster than the physics-based IFS ENS, with approximately 1,000× lower energy consumption per forecast.
- A single AIFS ensemble member running on one NVIDIA A100 GPU produces a 15-day forecast in roughly 2.5 minutes, compared to ~1 hour on 96 AMD Epyc Rome CPUs for an equivalent physics-based ensemble member.
- Known limitations include artefacts in mean sea level pressure, reduced effective resolution in cloud fields, and spurious patterns in accumulated precipitation — all active research areas.
- At ~30 km resolution, AIFS ENS is coarser than the ~9 km IFS ENS, which can affect representation of extreme precipitation events and high-resolution features.
- Open Data distribution is a curated subset. Higher-resolution AIFS ENS products may be available via ECMWF's Real-time Dissemination Service under separate agreements.
