# C-LAEF (Convection-permitting Limited-Area Ensemble Forecasting)

## What this model is
C-LAEF (Convection-permitting Limited-Area Ensemble Forecasting) is the operational convection-permitting regional ensemble prediction system run by GeoSphere Austria over Austria and the surrounding Alpine region. It is the probabilistic, AROME-based companion to the deterministic [AROME Austria](../../../nwp_models/regional/austria/arome-austria.md) (AROME-Aut), sharing the same 2.5 km / L90 grid, dynamical core, and physics.

C-LAEF quantifies forecast uncertainty for short-range, small-scale and rapidly evolving weather — thunderstorms, heavy precipitation, fog, foehn and local wind effects — in complex Alpine terrain. Its control member is configured identically to AROME-Aut and runs on separate hardware, so it can serve as an operational backup for the deterministic system.

---

## Who runs it
- **Organization:** GeoSphere Austria (formerly ZAMG — Zentralanstalt für Meteorologie und Geodynamik; merged into GeoSphere Austria in 2023)
- **Country / region:** Austria

---

## What area it covers
- **Coverage:** Austria and the surrounding (extended) Alpine region
- **Domain details:** 600 × 432 grid points centred on the Alpine region (same domain as the deterministic AROME-Aut). The public dataset is published on a WGS84 grid (EPSG:4326) with a bounding box of approximately 42.98–51.82 °N, 5.49–22.1 °E.

---

## Basic details
- **Model type:** Ensemble NWP (convection-permitting)
- **Model system / core:** AROME-based EPS (ALADIN-NH non-hydrostatic spectral dynamical core), code cycle **cy43t2bf11**
- **Dynamical formulation:** Non-hydrostatic, spectral, with semi-Lagrangian advection and semi-implicit time integration
- **Convection-allowing:** Yes (deep convection explicitly resolved at 2.5 km; shallow convection parameterized)
- **Ensemble size:** 16 perturbed members + 1 control (17 total)
- **Horizontal resolution:** ~2.5 km
- **Grid dimensions:** 600 × 432 grid points
- **Vertical levels:** 90 (lowest level ~5 m above ground; model top ~35 km)
- **Forecast length:** 60 hours (00 and 12 UTC long runs); intermediate cycles run only to ~+3 h to close the assimilation cycle
- **Update frequency / cycles:** 8× daily on a 3-hourly cycle (00, 03, …, 21 UTC); full +60 h forecasts at 00 and 12 UTC
- **Time step:** 60 s
- **Temporal output resolution:** 1 hour (both 2D and 3D fields)
- **Surface scheme:** SURFEX 8.0
- **Orography / physiography:** GMTED2010 / ECOCLIMAP 1

---

## Data assimilation
- **Data assimilation:** Yes — Ensemble Data Assimilation (EDA)
- **Method / cadence:** Ensemble **3D-Var** (with a Jk large-scale constraint term) for upper-air and ensemble **optimal interpolation / CANARI** for surface, cycled every 3 hours with a ±90 minute assimilation window. The control member uses the identical 3D-Var / OI setup as AROME-Aut.
- **Background-error covariance:** C-LAEF EDA climatological B-matrix
- **Observations assimilated:** Conventional surface (SYNOP, SHIP, TAWES), aircraft (Mode-S, AMDAR), radiosonde (BUFR TEMP), atmospheric motion vectors (SEVIRI-HR / GeoWind), ceilometer cloud cover (converted to RH; introduced in the September 2023 upgrade), and ASCAT scatterometer winds. *(The more extensive observation set — satellite radiances, weather radar, GNSS-ZTD, GNSS-RO, wind profilers — is used by the higher-resolution AROME-RUC nowcasting system, not by C-LAEF/AROME-Aut.)*

---

## Initial and boundary conditions
- **Initial conditions:** Per-member ensemble 3D-Var + Jk (upper-air) and ensemble OI (surface), drawn from the C-LAEF EDA. The control member is initialized like AROME-Aut (3D-Var / OI).
- **Boundary conditions:** ECMWF **IFS ENS** (the first 16 perturbed members drive the 16 perturbed C-LAEF members) plus **IFS HRES** (driving the control member), updated hourly.

---

## Perturbations and design
- **Initial-condition perturbations:** Generated by the C-LAEF Ensemble Data Assimilation (EDA) — each member is initialized from a separate perturbed ensemble 3D-Var analysis.
- **Model/physics perturbations:** A pure **Stochastic Parameter Perturbation (SPP)** scheme (operational since September 2023), which replaced an earlier hybrid scheme that acted on physics tendencies and selected parameters.
- **Surface perturbations:** A surface perturbation scheme (introduced December 2021) improves the skill/spread ratio for near-surface parameters.
- **Boundary perturbations:** Inherited from the driving ECMWF IFS ENS perturbed members, keeping initial-condition and lateral-boundary perturbations dynamically consistent.

---

## What it provides
The operational ensemble generates 16+1 member forecasts together with derived ensemble products (ensemble mean and spread, probability maps, and Met/EPSgrams) for parameters including 2 m temperature and humidity, 10 m wind and gusts, precipitation, cloud cover, radiation, and thunderstorm/convection indices.

The **publicly distributed dataset** (`ensemble-v1-1h-2500m`, "Ensemble Forecast for Austria") is a reduced product: it contains the **10th, 50th and 90th percentiles** computed from the 17 underlying runs (16 perturbed members + 1 control), at hourly resolution out to +60 h, for temperature, precipitation, wind, radiation, cloud cover, and thunderstorm parameters. Individual members are not distributed.

## Notes
- **Public data is a subset of the operational ensemble.** Only the P10/P50/P90 percentile fields are distributed, not the individual 16+1 members. Users needing raw members or additional parameters should contact GeoSphere Austria directly.
- **Relationship to AROME-Aut:** The C-LAEF control run and the deterministic [AROME Austria](../../../nwp_models/regional/austria/arome-austria.md) are configured identically but run on different HPC platforms, so the control can act as an operational backup for AROME-Aut. As of 2026, GeoSphere documentation increasingly describes the deterministic run simply as "the C-LAEF control mirrored on the local HPC."
- **Hardware:** The full C-LAEF ensemble runs on the **ECMWF ATOS XH2000** system (as a TC2 special-project application). The control run is mirrored on GeoSphere Austria's on-site **HPE CRAY-XD2000** cluster (operational since 15 January 2025; it replaced the earlier HPE Apollo 8600).
- **Reanalysis spin-off (ARA):** The high-resolution Austrian Re-analysis ensemble (**ARA**) is built on the C-LAEF system at 2.5 km / L90 with 10+1 members and 3-hourly cycling, targeting 2013–2023 with ERA5 coupling. ARA is a research/reanalysis product, not part of this operational entry.
- **Relationship to the wider AROME family:** C-LAEF shares its dynamical core and physics with the broader AROME / HARMONIE-AROME family operated by ACCORD-consortium services. See [AROME Austria](../../../nwp_models/regional/austria/arome-austria.md), [AROME France](../../../nwp_models/regional/france/arome-france.md), [MEPS](../../../nwp_models/regional/norway/meps.md), and [AROME-Arctic](../../../nwp_models/regional/norway/arome-arctic.md) for related deployments.
- **Organizational note:** GeoSphere Austria was formed by the 2023 merger of ZAMG with the Geological Survey of Austria; older publications refer to ZAMG.

## Recent version history

### Forward look — C-LAEF AlpeAdria (1 km successor)
C-LAEF is expected to be replaced during summer 2026 by **C-LAEF AlpeAdria**, a 1 km ensemble developed jointly with ARSO (Slovenia) and DHMZ (Croatia), running on ECMWF ATOS in lagged mode with quadratic spectral truncation and a southward-extended domain. It uses newer cycles (cy46t1+ / cy48t3bf3 for its EnVar member) and includes a 3D-EnVar member supporting direct reflectivity assimilation. (This is the matured form of the system previously developed under the name "C-LAEF 1k.")

### 2025-01-15 — Control run migrated to new on-site HPC
The locally mirrored C-LAEF control run moved to GeoSphere Austria's new **HPE CRAY-XD2000** cluster. The full perturbed ensemble continues to run on ECMWF ATOS. This was a hardware change rather than a model upgrade.

### September 2023 — SPP scheme and ceilometer assimilation
The hybrid model-physics perturbation scheme (acting on tendencies and selected parameters) was replaced by a pure **Stochastic Parameter Perturbation (SPP)** scheme. **Ceilometer** cloud-cover observations (converted to RH) were introduced into the ensemble 3D-Var, improving low-stratus forecasts in autumn and winter.

### December 2021 — Major upgrade to cycle cy43t2
C-LAEF was upgraded from cy40t1 to cy43t2 alongside AROME-Aut and AROME-RUC. The control member was aligned to the identical AROME-Aut setup (enabling backup capability), the assimilation cycle was shortened from 6 h to 3 h, and a surface perturbation scheme was added to improve the near-surface skill/spread ratio. C-LAEF also inherited the AROME-Aut changes (adapted screening-level diagnostics, new B-matrix / REDNMC tuning, GTOPO30 → GMTED2010 orography, and additional output parameters).

### November 2019 — Operational start
C-LAEF became operational as an AROME-based EPS on the ECMWF HPC facility, initially running four times a day (long +60 h / +48 h runs at 00/12 UTC and short runs at 06/18 UTC to close a 6-hour assimilation cycle).
