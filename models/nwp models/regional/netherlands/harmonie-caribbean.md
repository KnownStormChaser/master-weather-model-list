# HARMONIE-AROME Caribbean

## What this model is
A high-resolution regional weather forecast model designed to predict conditions such as temperature, wind, precipitation, and pressure over the Caribbean region, with particular emphasis on the Dutch Caribbean (Bonaire, St. Eustatius, and Saba) and adjacent areas.

It is part of the KNMI operational HARMONIE-AROME Cy43 suite, run jointly under the United Weather Centres-West (UWC-West) partnership.

---

## Who runs it
- **Organization:** : Royal Netherlands Meteorological Institute
- **Country / region:** Netherlands (Caribbean domain)

---

## What area it covers
- **Coverage:** Caribbean region including the Dutch Caribbean, Windward & Leeward Islands, and parts of northern South America.

---

## Basic details
- **Model type:** Regional (HARMONIE-AROME based)
- **Typical resolution:** ~0.05° (~5.5–6 km) for regional domain (approximate)
- **Forecast length:** Up to ~60 hours
- **Update frequency:** Hourly output where available, generated from twice-daily deterministic cycles

## Notes
This regional HARMONIE domain extends beyond Europe and is particularly useful for forecasting tropical weather, including potential hurricanes and convection-dominated regimes in the Caribbean.
