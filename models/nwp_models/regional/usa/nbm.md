# NBM (National Blend of Models)

## What this model is
The National Blend of Models (NBM) is a weather forecast system that combines and blends forecasts from many different weather models into a single, consistent set of predictions.

Instead of running its own atmospheric simulation, it statistically blends outputs from global and regional models.

---

## Who runs it
- **Organization:** : NOAA / National Weather Service
- **Country / region:** United States

---

## What area it covers
- **Coverage:** United States and surrounding areas

---

## Basic details
- **Model type:** Regional (multi-model blend)
- **Typical resolution:** ~2.5 km
- **Forecast length:** Up to 7 days (varies by variable)
- **Update frequency:** Hourly

---

## Data availability
- **Is the data free?** Yes
- **Is the data downloadable?** Yes
- **Data formats:** GRIB2
- **Official download location:**  
  https://registry.opendata.aws/noaa-nbm/

  https://vlab.noaa.gov/web/mdl/nbm-download

---

## Notes
NBM is widely used for producing official National Weather Service forecasts.  
Because it blends many models, it often provides smoother and more consistent forecasts than any single model.
