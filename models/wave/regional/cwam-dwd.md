# CWAM (Coastal Wave Model)

## What this model is
CWAM is a high-resolution coastal wave forecast model operated by the German Weather Service. It predicts detailed wave conditions in coastal and nearshore waters.

It is optimized for short-range forecasting in shallow and complex coastal environments.

---

## Who runs it
- **Organization:** : Deutscher Wetterdienst
- **Country / region:** Germany

---

## What area it covers
- **Coverage:** Coastal seas around Germany  
  (including the North Sea and Baltic Sea)

---

## Basic details
- **Model type:** Wave model
- **Typical resolution:** ~0.01° (high resolution)
- **Forecast length:** Up to ~3–4 days
- **Update frequency:** 2× daily

---

## What it predicts
- Significant wave height
- Mean and peak wave period
- Mean wave direction
- Coastal wave parameters influenced by shallow water effects

---

## Data availability
- **Is the data free?** Yes
- **Is the data downloadable?** Yes
- **Data formats:** GRIB2, NetCDF
- **Official download location:**  
  https://opendata.dwd.de/weather/maritime/wave_models/cwam/

---

## Notes
CWAM is intended for detailed coastal and nearshore applications such as shipping safety, port operations, and coastal hazard assessment.
