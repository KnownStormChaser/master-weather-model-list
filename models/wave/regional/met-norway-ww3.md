# WAVEWATCH III (WW3) – MET Norway

## What this model is
MET Norway’s WAVEWATCH III (WW3) is an operational wave forecast model that predicts ocean wave conditions such as significant wave height, wave period, and wave direction.

It is particularly well suited for the North Atlantic, Nordic seas, and Arctic regions.

---

## Who runs it
- **Organization:** : Met Norway
- **Country / region:** Norway

---

## What area it covers
- **Coverage:**  
  - North Atlantic  
  - Nordic seas  
  - Arctic Ocean  
  - Regional nested domains around Norway and Svalbard

---

## Basic details
- **Model type:** Wave model
- **Typical resolution:** ~0.1°–0.25° (varies by domain)
- **Forecast length:** Up to ~7 days
- **Update frequency:** 4× daily

---

## What it predicts
- Significant wave height
- Mean and peak wave period
- Mean wave direction
- Wind-wave and swell components (varies by product)

---

## Data availability
- **Is the data free?** Yes
- **Is the data downloadable?** Yes
- **Data formats:** NetCDF (THREDDS), GRIB2 (selected products)
- **Official download location:**  
  https://thredds.met.no/

---

## Notes
MET Norway’s wave forecasts are based on the WAVEWATCH III model and are widely used for marine forecasting, research, and Arctic operations.

The open data portal provides easy access to both global and regional wave model outputs.
