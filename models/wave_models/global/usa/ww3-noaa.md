# WAVEWATCH III (WW3) – NOAA

## What this model is
WAVEWATCH III (WW3) is a global wave forecast model that predicts ocean wave conditions such as significant wave height, wave period, and wave direction.

It uses wind forecasts from atmospheric models to simulate how waves form, grow, and move across the oceans.

---

## Who runs it
- **Organization:** : National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration
- **Country / region:** United States

---

## What area it covers
- **Coverage:** Global oceans  
- Regional nested domains are also produced by NOAA and other agencies

---

## Basic details
- **Model type:** Wave model
- **Typical resolution:** ~0.25° (varies by domain)
- **Forecast length:** Up to ~10 days
- **Update frequency:** 4× daily

---

## What it predicts
- Significant wave height
- Wave period
- Wave direction
- Swell and wind-wave components (varies by product)

---

---

## Notes
WAVEWATCH III is one of the most widely used wave models in the world and serves as the foundation for many national and international wave forecasting systems.

Many weather services run their own regional wave models based on WW3.
